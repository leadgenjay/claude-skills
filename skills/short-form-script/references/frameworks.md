# Short-Form Script Frameworks — Lead Gen Jay

Reference doc for the `short-form-script` skill. Covers voice rules, hook formulas, script structures, and psychology.

---

## 1. Jay's Voice Rules

**Positioning:** Peer-to-peer authority — "successful friend sharing insider knowledge." NOT teacher-to-student.

| Rule | Do | Don't |
|------|-----|-------|
| Tone | Confident, conversational, results-focused | Preachy, lecture-y, "let me teach you" |
| Contractions | "I'm gonna", "Watch this", "Here's the thing" | "I am going to", "Allow me to demonstrate" |
| Authority | Reference personal results, client wins, named tools | Vague "in my experience" or generic advice |
| Social proof | Specific metrics: "47 meetings in first month", "2700+ businesses served" | "Many clients have seen results" |
| Business language | Scale, conversion, ROI, leads, revenue, systems | Jargon without context, buzzwords |
| Name drops | "Just like Russell Brunson teaches...", "I learned this from my $1M client..." | Namedropping without relevance |
| Content focus | Cold outreach, client acquisition, revenue, automation, GoHighLevel, Instantly, LinkedIn/Aimfox, "selling shovels" | Generic self-help, motivation-only content |
| Audience | Young entrepreneurs (18-45) building businesses | Corporate executives, academics |

**Anti-patterns:**
- No overly polished/promotional messaging
- No teacher-student dynamic ("Today class, we'll learn...")
- No generic advice without specific metrics
- No "educational" framing — frame as insider sharing

## 2. The 4 Hook Types

Every hook has 4 components that must align. Misalignment = viewer confusion = scroll.

| Hook Type | What It Is | Example |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **Spoken** | First 1-3 sentences you say on camera | "I just replaced an entire marketing team using Claude Code." |
| **Text overlay** | Bold on-screen text (1-2 lines max) | REPLACED MY MARKETING TEAM WITH AI |
| **Visual** | Camera position, what's on screen, movement | Direct-to-camera, confident lean-in, phone in hand |
| **Audio** | Music, SFX, silence cues | Tension drone → impact bass hit on "Claude Code" |

**Alignment rules:**
- Text overlay must SIMPLIFY the spoken hook, not repeat it verbatim
- Visual must support the claim (show the tool, show the result, show the reaction)
- Audio must match the emotional beat (shock = bass hit, curiosity = rising synth, authority = confident music)

## 3. 3-Step Business Hook Formula

Adapted from Kallaway's hook methodology for business/tech niche:

### Step 1: Business Context Lean-In (0.5-1s)
Set the scene with a recognizable business pain or context. The viewer self-identifies.
- "Cold email is dead."
- "You sent 10,000 emails last month."
- "Every agency owner I know..."

### Step 2: Scroll-Stop Interjection (0.5-1s)
Pattern interrupt that creates a curiosity gap. The unexpected element.
- "...and I don't care."
- "...zero replies."
- "...is doing this wrong."

### Step 3: Authority Snapback (1-2s)
Re-anchor with credibility. The viewer stays because you've earned the next 30 seconds.
- "Here's what I did instead."
- "I built a system that fixes this."
- "Watch what happens when I click this."

**Combined example:**
"Everyone's sending cold emails the same way. *(lean-in)* They're all landing in spam. *(scroll-stop)* I built a machine that books 47 meetings a month on autopilot. Watch this. *(snapback)*"

## 4. Hook Structures

| Structure | Pattern | Best For | Example Framework |
|-----------|---------|----------|-------------------|
| **Educational** | "Here's how to [X]" / "I'm gonna show you [X]" | Tutorials, demos, how-tos | "I'm gonna show you how to [outcome] using [tool] in less than [time]." |
| **Secret Reveal** | "[Hidden thing] that [surprising result]" | Insider knowledge, tools, methods | "There's a [tool/method] that [big claim] and nobody's talking about it." |
| **Contrarian** | "[Common belief] is wrong/dead" | Hot takes, myth busting | "[Popular thing] is dead. Here's what's replacing it." |
| **Raw Shock** | Emotional reaction, extreme claim | Attention grabbing, viral potential | "[X] is ruining my life. For the last [time], I basically haven't been able to [Y]." |
| **Question** | Direct question that demands mental answer | Engagement, comparison | "Did you know you can [surprising capability]?" |
| **FOMO** | Urgency, exclusivity, scarcity | Launches, limited offers, trends | "Someone just leaked [valuable thing] and it's only available [constraint]." |
| **Comparison** | "[X] vs [Y]" with clear winner | Tool reviews, method comparisons | "This is [good], this is [better], and this is [best]." |
| **Experimentation** | "I tested/tried [X]" with results | Proof-based content | "I tested [method] for [time] and here's what happened." |
| **Curiosity Gap** | Tease without revealing | Story-driven, discovery content | "You might not know this, but you can actually [surprising thing]." |

## 5. 4 Hook Mistakes

| Mistake | What Happens | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| **Delay** | Hook starts after 3 seconds | Front-load the scroll-stop. First word = most important word. |
| **Confusion** | Viewer doesn't know what the video is about | One clear topic per video. If you can't explain it in 5 words, simplify. |
| **Irrelevance** | Hook doesn't match the body content | Write hook AFTER the body. The hook is a promise — the body must deliver. |
| **Disinterest** | Hook is accurate but boring | Use specific numbers, name real tools, reference real results. Generic = scroll. |

## 6. Script Structure

### Packaging (pre-hook)
Before filming, lock:
- **Title** — Results-driven, addresses a pain point (for internal reference + text overlay)
- **Value proposition** — One sentence: what the viewer gets from watching

### Hook (0-3 seconds)
- 3-step business hook formula (Context Lean-In → Scroll-Stop → Authority Snapback)
- Under 20 words spoken
- Text overlay on screen immediately

### Body (varies by duration)
- **15s:** 1 high-impact point + CTA
- **30s:** 2 points + CTA
- **60s:** 2 points + re-hook at midpoint + CTA
- **90s:** 3 points + 2 re-hooks + CTA

Each point follows: **Setup → Payoff**
- Setup: Context, mini case study, personal example, or pain identification
- Payoff: The strategy, result, or insight (with specific metric)

### CTA (last 3-5 seconds)
- Matches funnel stage (see CTA Templates below)
- Natural transition from final point — not bolted on
- Lead magnet tie-in when using comment/DM triggers

## 7. 3-Beat Rule (Demos)

For demo/tutorial content, every demonstration follows:

| Beat | Purpose | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Setup** | What we're about to do + why it matters | "I'm going to show you how to get 500 leads in 60 seconds." |
| **Magic** | The impressive moment (screen recording, live demo) | *Shows clicking one button → leads populate* |
| **Result** | The proof that it worked + what it means | "537 verified leads. That's $0.002 per lead." |

**Rule:** Never show the magic without the setup (no context = no impact). Never show the setup without the result (no payoff = no CTA).

## 8. Body Frameworks by Content Type

### Demo (show a tool/process in action)
```
Context → "Here's the problem most people have with [X]"
Impressive thing → Live screen recording / demo of the solution
Proof → Show the result (metrics, output, before/after)
Payoff → What this means for the viewer + CTA
```

### Tutorial (teach a specific skill/method)
```
Promise → "By the end of this, you'll know how to [outcome]"
Key step(s) → The actual method (1-2 steps max for short-form)
Result → Show or reference what happens when you do this
CTA → "Follow for part 2" or "Comment [keyword] for the full guide"
```

### Everything Else (hot takes, stories, rants, news)
```
Setup → Bold claim or provocative statement
Twist → The unexpected angle or evidence
Evidence → Specific data, personal experience, or example
Close → Restate the thesis + CTA
```

## 9. Re-hook Phrases

For 30-60s videos, insert a re-hook at the midpoint to retain viewers who might scroll:

| Type | Phrase | When |
|------|--------|------|
| **Tease** | "But here's where it gets interesting..." | Before revealing the key insight |
| **Stakes** | "And this is the part most people mess up." | Before the main teaching point |
| **Pattern interrupt** | "Wait. Watch this." | Before a screen recording or demo |
| **Direct address** | "If you're still watching, you're already ahead." | After a dense section |
| **Promise** | "The next 15 seconds will save you [X]." | Midpoint of 60s videos |

## 10. Writing Rules

| Rule | Spec |
|------|------|
| Reading level | 6th grade vocabulary. Simple words, short sentences. |
| Voice | Active voice only. "I built" not "It was built." |
| Hook length | Under 20 words spoken. |
| Specificity | Specific metrics, not generic claims. "47 meetings" not "lots of meetings." |
| Contractions | Always. "I'm gonna" not "I am going to." |
| Filler words | Zero. No "basically," "literally," "actually," "just" (unless intentional rhythm). |
| Sentence length | Max 15 words per sentence in the script. |
| One idea | One idea per sentence. One topic per video. |
| Banned AI words | See CLAUDE.md banned words list — never use them. |
| Numbers | Use digits in text overlays ("47"), spell out in spoken ("forty-seven"). |

## 11. Psychology Checkpoints

Validate internally — never expose these in the output. Every script must pass all 6:

| Checkpoint | Question | If No |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Pain Acceptance** | Does the viewer recognize their problem in the first 3 seconds? | Rewrite hook with more specific pain. |
| **Trust** | Is there a credibility signal in the first 10 seconds? | Add metric, client reference, or tool name. |
| **Plan of Attack** | Does the viewer understand what to do next? | Simplify the body to clearer steps. |
| **Likability** | Does Jay come across as a peer, not a lecturer? | Soften tone, add conversational aside. |
| **Attention** | Is there a re-hook or pattern interrupt before attention drops? | Add midpoint re-hook phrase. |
| **Action** | Is the CTA specific and easy? | Simplify to one action: comment, DM, or follow. |

## 12. CTA Templates

### Comment Trigger (primary for IG Reels)
```
Spoken: "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send you [lead magnet]."
Text overlay: COMMENT "[KEYWORD]" BELOW
Visual: Point down gesture or arrow graphic
Lead magnet: Free guide, template, checklist, or video
```

### Follow
```
Spoken: "Follow me for more [topic] breakdowns every week."
Text overlay: FOLLOW @LEADGENJAY
Visual: Profile card or handle display
```

### Link in Bio
```
Spoken: "Link in bio to get [specific thing]."
Text overlay: LINK IN BIO
Visual: Tap-through gesture or arrow to profile
```

### Save (engagement boost)
```
Spoken: "Save this for later — you're gonna need it."
Text overlay: SAVE THIS
Visual: Bookmark icon animation
```

## 13. Platform Pacing

| Platform | Sweet Spot Duration | Pacing | Tone | Notes |
|----------|-------------------|--------|------|-------|
| **IG Reels** | 30s, 60s | Fast, punchy | Direct, confident | Primary format. Max reach. 60s for tutorials, 30s for hot takes. |
| **TikTok** | 15s, 30s | Fastest | Casual, raw, aggressive hooks | More pattern interrupt, less polish. Lean into trends. |
| **YouTube Shorts** | 30s, 60s | Medium | Slightly more explanatory | Can afford 1-2 extra setup beats. |
| **Stories** | 15s | Fast, intimate | Direct-to-camera energy | 8-10 sentences max. |

**Duration → point count:**
| Duration | Points | Re-hooks | Total sentences (approx) |
|----------|--------|----------|--------------------------|
| 15s | 1 | 0 | 5-7 |
| 30s | 2 | 0 | 10-14 |
| 60s | 2 | 1 | 18-24 |
| 90s | 3 | 2 | 28-35 |
