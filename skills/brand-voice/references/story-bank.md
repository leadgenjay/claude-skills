# Jay's Story Bank

Each story is matched to specific lessons. Don't force a story into a lesson it doesn't earn.

## Personal Origin Stories

### Pooping My Pants at Camp (age 12)
**Scene:** Playing tetherball, suddenly had an accident. Hit himself in the forehead with the ball on purpose as a distraction so his friend would go find a counselor while he ran to the bathroom.
**Key details:** Yellow ball on a string. Disposed of underpants, shorts, shoes and socks. "Yes, it was everywhere."
**Lessons:**
- Use misdirection and distraction to solve problems
- The best solutions take creativity and quick thinking

### Girlfriend Cheating (age 18)
**Scene:** Stole fraternity brother's girlfriend (Hooters calendar girl, volleyball player, looked like Megan Fox). She was a "smokin hot 18 year old model." Found a text from a Red Sox player on her phone while she was in the shower at a cheap hotel during a volleyball tournament. "My heart dropped into my stomach." Walked out.
**Key details:** He was a pro beach volleyball player. She was getting attention from rich guys. Red Sox player in town for Spring Training.
**Lessons:**
- If someone does wrong to someone else even if it benefits you, they should not be trusted
- Build yourself into someone others can't compete with
- Get rich and build your confidence

### Getting Arrested (age 17)
**Scene:** Chased kids who were giving them the middle finger into their garage. Stole a bottle of raspberry vodka. The house belonged to a cop. Pulled out of school next day "wearing a pink cutoff shirt like an asshole." Confessed to everything. Juvenile detention. Dad showed up in tears after 9 hours on a cold bench.
**Key details:** Charged with a felony. First time seeing his dad cry. Sitting on the cold bench for 9 hours.
**Lessons:**
- Something terrible can motivate you to change your life
- Controlling your emotions is critical
- Education, mentors, and preparation matter
- Sometimes bad luck happens and you break into the wrong house, or email the wrong person

### Dad Losing His Job
**Scene:** Dad was a CPA at a construction company for 20 years, worked his way up to CFO, $300K/year. 2007 housing collapse wiped it. Family filed for bankruptcy, lost the house, moved to a tiny home. Mom became addicted to anti-depressants, parents divorced. Jay had to work as a collections agent at his dad's new vegetable company to pay $10K in legal bills.
**Key details:** Company was GL Homes (now worth hundreds of millions). "Watching this happen to my father, the nicest man on earth, is what drives me."
**Lessons:**
- Make your business safe from changing economic conditions
- Own your own business so no one can fire you
- Don't spend money on stupid shit
- The "safe thing" (CPA, 9-5) isn't actually safe

### Collections Agent Shitty Job
**Scene:** 1980s office building "where fun goes to die." 5x5 cubicle making collections calls. "My fat bald boss, named Dick, would waddle over and yell at me." Only joy was spicy chicken nuggets from Wendy's. "Every night before work, I would lay in bed dreading the morning."
**Key details:** Drove 30 minutes each way. Couldn't quit because family needed the money. Still thinks about the cubicle every day.
**Lessons:**
- The only way to avoid a shitty boss is to work for yourself
- Most people work 9-5 jobs they hate and party on weekends with the other 99%
- Hopelessness is temporary, there's always a way out

### Getting Rejected from Medical School
**Scene:** Great MCAT scores, 3.8 GPA. Applied to every MD school in Florida. "Crickets... not a single school wanted to interview me." Found a mentor, spent a year doing research and retaking MCAT. Got into top choice in NYC on second try.
**Key details:** Problem was no research and average MCAT for his ethnicity. "Having a mentor would have saved me an entire year."
**Lessons:**
- Mentors identify blind spots and save you time/money/failures
- When something doesn't work, you're probably missing 1-2 things. Seek help.
- Don't give up until it works

## Business Stories

### Essay Chief ($9/hr to stashing cash)
**Scene:** After med school rejection, moved to Tallahassee. Medical scribe at $9/hr working overnight shifts. Started writing college papers. "FSU Greek life really didn't like writing papers." Best friend Jesse helped file an LLC. Ordered 10K business cards, put them on windshields at 5am. Hired VAs from Fiverr. One plagiarized a paper and nearly got a client expelled.
**Key details:** $50/page. "Would take an Adderall in the middle of the night to cram someone's 10 page paper." Cash stuffed in bedside safe. Business died when med school started because no online marketing knowledge.
**Lessons:**
- Can't be afraid to get hands dirty marketing
- Even trusted people can cut corners - trust but verify
- Find a mentor a couple steps ahead of you

### Instelite ($40K/mo that crashed)
**Scene:** Built IG automation company during 3rd/4th year of med school. 500 profiles at $97/mo. Bought a Jaguar. "All the accounts were running on servers in my living room." Instagram started suspending accounts. Faced lawsuits. Tried fighting with dongles and mobile SIM cards. Sold 50% stake to friends, which became worthless.
**Key details:** 90% margins, single VA. "$40K/mo to nothing." Pivoted to Otter PR and got back to $50K/mo in 6 months.
**Lessons:**
- When your business depends on another company letting you operate, you are vulnerable
- Don't wait to pivot when conditions change
- Get insurance and signed contracts from clients

### Otter PR ($600K/mo, Inc 5000)
**Scene:** Started in 2020 with Scott Bartnick. Saw gap: legacy PR firms charging six-figure retainers without delivering. Offered month-to-month contracts with guaranteed coverage. Built to 60+ employees, Inc 5000.
**Key details:** Down to last $1,000 in 2021. COVID timing as unfair advantage. "Fastest-growing PR firm in the country."
**Lessons:**
- Product-market fit is essential before scaling
- Partnership with complementary skills (Visionary + Integrator per "Rocket Fuel")
- Build a good business slowly rather than mediocre business quickly

### E-Commerce Failures (Rave Doctor, REX, Life Vine, HydroRX)
**Scene:** Multiple brands, most failed. Rave Doctor succeeded (highest rated EDM supplement on Amazon). REX blocked by the ship stuck in the Panama Canal. HydroRX packets didn't rip properly. Life Vine got undercut on price.
**Lessons:**
- No such thing as passive income
- Even the best entrepreneurs have failures
- When entering a new sector, find a mentor

### The Diarrhea Story (girlfriend's pre-workout)
**Scene:** Girlfriend had a "persistent plumbing problem" for a year. Jay went "full Sherlock." Culprit was pre-workout powder with gut-wrecking artificial sweeteners. "Two weeks off the powder plus some probiotics, and voila!"
**Key details:** She was too proud to ask for help. He tracked her diet, meds, everything.
**Lessons:**
- Business blindspots are costing you more than you know
- If you're pulling in less than $10K, you've got blindspots bigger than the Titanic's iceberg
- Sometimes you need an outside perspective
