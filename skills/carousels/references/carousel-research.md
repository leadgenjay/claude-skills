# Instagram Carousel Research

Research from @marketingharry's viral posts (2500+ carousels, 8K+ avg likes) and Instagram best practices for 2026.

## Dimensions

| Format | Dimensions | Use Case |
|--------|------------|----------|
| **3:4 (best)** | 1080x1440 | Organic posts -- maximum feed real estate in 2026 |
| 4:5 | 1080x1350 | Boosted/paid posts -- required for ad placements |
| 1:1 | 1080x1080 | Avoid -- wastes vertical space |

**Key insight:** 3:4 replaced 4:5 as the optimal organic format in 2025-2026. It takes up more vertical screen space in the feed, increasing dwell time and engagement.

## Safe Zones (Critical for Reels Tab)

Carousels with music appear in the Reels tab. Safe zones prevent UI elements from covering content:

| Edge | Safe Zone | Reason |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Top | 180px | Username, follow button overlay |
| Bottom | 180px | Caption, like/comment/share buttons |
| Left | 50px | Edge proximity |
| Right | 120px | Share/save buttons, scroll indicator |

**All critical text and visuals must be within these safe zones.**

## Typography

- Headlines: 50pt+ minimum (55pt shown in top-performing posts)
- Body: 14pt+ minimum
- Must be readable "even when squinting" on small/dim screens
- High contrast: 8.54+ contrast ratio = "very good" readability
- Bold condensed fonts for headlines (Big Shoulders Black)
- Clean sans-serif for body (Manrope)

## Design Principles

1. **Follow Western reading patterns** -- Numbered visual hierarchy: top-left to right to down. Guide the eye with numbered elements, arrows, or visual flow
2. **Integrated imagery** -- Images blended into layout with shadows, depth, and context. Not flat/pasted on top like stickers
3. **Clean cutouts** -- Professional background removal when using photos. No ugly manual cuts or harsh edges
4. **High contrast** -- 8.54+ contrast ratio is the target. White text on near-black (#0D0D0D) achieves this naturally
5. **Consistent visual language** -- Same background, typography, accent colors across all slides. Each slide should feel like part of a cohesive set

## Carousel Framework (Slide Structure)

| Slide | Purpose | Content |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 1 (Cover) | Stop the scroll | Bold hook headline + creator photo + "Swipe" CTA |
| 2-3 | Build interest | Example, story, or problem statement |
| 4-6 | Retain attention | Diagrams, visuals, practical info |
| 7-8 | Deliver value | Actionable tips, frameworks |
| 9-10 | CTA | Follow CTA + offer/lead magnet |

**Why 8-10 slides:** Instagram's algorithm rewards time-on-post. More slides = more swipes = more dwell time = more reach. Posts with 8-10 slides consistently outperform shorter carousels.

## Engagement Patterns

- **Cover slide** determines 80% of performance -- it must stop the scroll
- **Slide 2** must hook immediately -- if they don't swipe to slide 3, the carousel is dead
- **Mid-carousel** (slides 4-6) is where most people drop off -- use visuals/diagrams to retain
- **CTA slide** should be simple and direct -- one clear action

## Hook Formulas That Work

1. "X [things] that [outcome]" (listicle)
2. "[Bold claim]. Here's proof." (case study)
3. "Stop doing [common mistake]" (myth-busting)
4. "[Old way] vs [New way]" (comparison)
5. "The [framework name] system" (framework)
6. "How to [outcome] in [timeframe]" (how-to)

## What NOT to Do

- Don't use 1:1 format (wastes vertical space)
- Don't put text outside safe zones
- Don't use thin fonts for headlines (not readable at mobile size)
- Don't make every slide text-only (mix visuals)
- Don't skip the swipe CTA on the cover
- Don't use more than 2 accent-colored words per slide
- Don't paste images flat without shadows/depth
- Don't use low-contrast color combinations
- Don't go below 8 slides (too short for algorithm)
- Don't exceed 15 words per slide (cognitive overload)
