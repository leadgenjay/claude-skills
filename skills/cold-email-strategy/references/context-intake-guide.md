# Context Intake Guide

## Material Types & What to Extract

### Call Transcripts (Fathom, Otter, manual notes)
- **How the founder describes the offer** in their own words (this is gold for copy)
- **Objections prospects raise** on sales calls and how they're handled
- **Specific results or metrics** mentioned casually ("we saved them 40 hours a month")
- **Language patterns** — phrases the founder repeats, vocabulary they use naturally
- **Pain points prospects describe** — their words, not the founder's summary
- **Competitive mentions** — who prospects compare them to, what alternatives they've tried

### Google Docs / Strategy Notes
- **Positioning decisions** already made (target market, pricing, differentiation)
- **ICP hypotheses** — who they think the customer is (validate against product-vs-customer trap)
- **Case studies or results** documented anywhere in the doc
- **Messaging they've already tested** — what worked, what didn't
- **Constraints or requirements** — compliance, brand guidelines, tone preferences

### Sales Call Recordings / Summaries
- Same extraction as transcripts, plus:
- **Tone of voice** — formal vs casual, fast vs measured, technical vs plain
- **Closing patterns** — how they ask for the sale (maps to CTA style)
- **Discovery questions they ask** — reveals what they think matters most

### Slide Decks / Pitch Materials
- **Value proposition** as they present it (often more polished than verbal)
- **Social proof slides** — logos, testimonials, metrics, case study snapshots
- **Pricing structure** — tiers, packages, anchor pricing
- **Competitive positioning** — how they frame alternatives
- **Visual brand tone** — professional, playful, technical, minimalist

### Competitor Research / Analysis
- **What competitors say** in their cold emails (if available)
- **Positioning gaps** — what competitors don't claim
- **Pricing benchmarks** — where the offer sits relative to market
- **Feature comparison** — unique capabilities vs table-stakes features

### Existing Email Copy (past campaigns, templates)
- **Tone baseline** — how they've written before (match or intentionally shift)
- **What got replies** — any A/B test data or anecdotal winners
- **What flopped** — patterns to avoid
- **Personalization approach** — how specific they've gone before
- **CTA patterns** — what they've asked prospects to do

## Processing Rules

1. **Extract, don't summarize.** Pull exact quotes and specific data points, not paraphrased summaries.
2. **Flag contradictions.** If a transcript says "we target enterprise" but the doc says "SMB focus," surface it for the user to resolve.
3. **Prioritize founder voice.** When the founder describes the offer differently across materials, use the most natural/conversational version for copy.
4. **Numbers are sacred.** Any specific metric, result, or data point gets preserved exactly as stated.
5. **Note what's missing.** After processing all materials, list what you still need to ask about.
