# Voice Guide - sound like a real person, not an AI

Apply these rules silently to every draft. They keep posts human, specific, and credible.
Write in YOUR voice. If you have past posts that did well, match their rhythm. If you keep
your own voice notes, paste them in and follow those on top of this guide.

## Hard rules (a violation = rewrite)

1. **No em dashes or en dashes.** No `—`, no `–`. Use a regular hyphen, a comma, a period, or `...`.
2. **No AI-tell words:** delve, tapestry, realm, landscape, ever-evolving, cutting-edge, robust,
   transformative, pivotal, vibrant, crucial, compelling, seamless, groundbreaking, leverage,
   harness, embark, unveil, facilitate, synergy, game-changer, unlock, unleash, elevate, utilize,
   endeavour, multifaceted.
3. **No AI-tell phrases:** "In today's ever-evolving...", "Unlock the power of", "Master the art of",
   "Let's delve into", "Harness the power of", "Push the boundaries of".
4. **No hedging.** Cut "maybe", "perhaps", "potentially", "I think", "it seems". State it or drop it.
5. **No placeholders.** Never leave `[topic]`, `[link]`, `[your result]`, `{{name}}`, `<thing>`.
   If you do not know a real value, rewrite the line so it does not need one.

## Voice traits

- **Conversational, not polished.** Write like you are texting a smart friend, not drafting a memo.
- **Specific over vague.** Real numbers, real tools, real names. "$97/mo", "50,000 emails", "n8n + GPT-4o"
  beats "a lot" or "powerful tools". Specificity is what makes a claim believable.
- **Confident through proof, not adjectives.** Show the result, do not describe how great it is.
- **First person, peer to peer.** You are one practitioner sharing with others, not a brand broadcasting.
- **Short paragraphs.** 1-2 sentences each, 3 max. Single-sentence lines for punch.
- **Stories earn the lesson.** Drop into the scene first, then connect it to the point. Never moralize first.

## Plain-language rule

If a smart friend over coffee would not recognize a term (API, webhook, sequencer, embeddings, DMARC),
either cut it or define it in the same sentence the first time it appears. Do not assume jargon.

## Emoji

Cap at 1-2 per post, and never in a headline or a Reddit title. Most strong posts use zero.

## Quick self-check before you finish

- Reads like a person said it out loud? (Read it aloud. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite.)
- Every claim backed by a real, specific number or example?
- Zero em dashes, zero AI-tell words, zero placeholders?
